# Context Builder v2
## Deep company and market intelligence
## for any company — produces both core
## context files and deep-dive research files

---

## What This Skill Does
Researches any company thoroughly and
produces two layers of output:

Layer 1 — Core context files (updated):
The four required files every other skill
reads. Richer and more structured than v1.

Layer 2 — Deep-dive research files (new):
Seven additional files covering voice of
customer, persona depth, objection depth,
competitive intelligence, buying committee,
trigger events, and market gap analysis.

This is a comprehensive single research run.
It takes longer than v1 and uses more
resources. The output makes every downstream
skill — campaign writer, keyword finder,
landing page brief, orchestrator — produce
meaningfully better results because they
have deeper, more specific intelligence
to.

---

## Step 1 — Intake

Ask these questions all at once in one
message. Wait for answers before proceeding.

---
Before I research, I need a few details.
Answer all in one message:

1. COMPANY NAME and WEBSITE URL
   What company are we researching?

2. ADDITIONAL CONTEXT (optional)
   Anything you already know that should
   inform the research:
   - Specific products or product lines
     to focus on
   - Target markets or geographies
   - Known competitors to prioritize
   - Recent news or events worth knowing
   - Specific personas most important
     to this research
   (Say "none" to skip)

3. EXISTING FILES
   Do existing context files already exist
   for this company?
   - Yes — update and enrich them
   - No — create fresh from scratch
   - Yes but they are outdated — rebuild
     from scratch and archive the old ones

4. PRIORITY PERSONAS
   Which buyer personas matter most for
   this research? List up to three in
   priority order.
   (If unsure, say "figure it out from
   research"ill identify the
   most important personas from what
   I find)

## Rules This Skill Must Always Follow

Never use placeholder content in any
file — every section must contain real
research. If a section cannot be
populated from available sources, say
so explicitly and note what research
would be needed to fill it.

Always cite sources for quotes —
never attribute a quote without noting
where it came from.

Never invent metrics or statistics —
if a number cannot be verified from
research, do not include it.

The voice of customer file is the
hority output — if research
time is constrained, go deeper here
before anywhere else.

Always create the context/deep/ folder
before writing deep-dive files.

Always display the research plan and
wait for 'proceed' before starting.

Always display the completion summary
with key findings — the summary is
what tells the human whether the
research was successful before they
read every file.

Follow the file naming convention
in CLAUDE.md for all output files.

Read the active company name from
the first line of context/icp.md
if updating existing files.
